Chole Bature

Description

Chole Bhature (or Chana Bhatura) is a popular North Indian dish made with spicy chickpea curry (chole) and deep-fried leavened bread (bhature).

It’s a hearty and satisfying meal, commonly eaten for breakfast or brunch in Delhi and Punjab, but enjoyed across India at any time of day, from street stalls to home kitchens.

Ingredients

  • Refined flour
  • Sugar
  • Salt
  • Oil
  • Dry Yeast
  • water
  • semolina
  • Black Cardamom
  • Black peppercorns
  • Dry fenugreek leaves
  • Nutmeg
  • CLoves
  • Fenugreek Powder
  • Coriander Powder
  • Asafoetida
  • Red chilli Powder
  • Cumin Powder
  • Potato
  • Ginger

Steps

  1. In a parat, add refined flour, sugar, salt to taste, oil, dry yeast soaked in water, semolina soaked in water for 1 hour and mix well making sure everything is well
  2. Further, add water as required and knead the dough.
  3. Knead to the smooth and soft dough without putting much pressure.
  4. Grease the dough with oil, cover and rest for 1 hours.
  5. Pinch a ball sized dough and make a ball without any cracks.
  6. Roll slightly thick, applying oil to prevent it from sticking.
  7. Cover and rest for 15 minutes.
  8. Finally, roll the dough evenly into circles using a rolling pin. roll neither too thin or thick.
  9. Heat oil in a deep kadai. When the oil is sufficiently hot, add one bhatura.
  10. Press with the spoon to puff up.
  11. Also once they begin to puff, splash oil over the bhatura till they puff completely.
  12. Flip over and fry the bhatura till golden brown all over.
  13. Then drain the bhature into tissue paper to remove excess oil. Fry all bhature the same way.
  14. In a bowl, add Black cardamom, Black peppercorns, Green cardamom, Mace, Dry fenugreek leaves.
  15. Add Cinnamon stick, Nutmeg, Star anise, Cloves, Fenugreek seeds.
  16. Transfer this into the pan and dry roast until the spices turn aromatic.
  17. Add coriander powder, A pinch of asafoetida, Degi red chilli powder, Cumin powder and dry roast well.
  18. Transfer it into a grinder jar and grind it finely. Keep it aside for further use.
  19. In a kadai, heat ghee, add pressure cooked chole, prepared chole masala, black tamarind water.
  20. Mix everything well to make sure the chole doesn’t break.
  21. Adjust consistency by adding remaining chole water.
  22. Simmer for 15-20 minutes on low flame, or until chole absorbs all the flavour.
  23. At the end, add ginger and ghee.
  24. Garnished with ginger, ghee, green chillies, fried aloo, sliced onion and coriander sprig.
  25. Serve hot with fried bhature.
